# PART B

## Question 1: Sourcing Methods

### 1. IndiaMART Manufacturer Directory

Why it works:
IndiaMART contains thousands of Indian manufacturers across biotech, chemicals, diagnostics, engineering, medical devices, specialty food ingredients, and industrial products. Manufacturer profiles often mention production facilities, certifications, machinery, and product categories.

Limitations:
Many traders and distributors are also listed, so manual filtering is required.

---

### 2. Export Import Data (Volza, Exim, ImportGenius)

Why it works:
Companies exporting specialty chemicals, biotech products, diagnostics, engineered components, or nutraceutical ingredients are usually real producers with operational infrastructure.

Limitations:
Exporting does not always imply differentiation. Some exporters are commodity businesses.

---

### 3. Industry Associations

Examples:

* Association of Biotechnology Led Enterprises (ABLE)
* Indian Drug Manufacturers Association (IDMA)
* Bulk Drug Manufacturers Association
* Specialty Chemical Associations
* Medical Technology Association of India (MTaI)

Why it works:
Members are usually active operators within the target industries.

Limitations:
Membership quality varies and some firms may not fit the ICP.

---

### 4. Government Databases

Examples:

* Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR)
* Startup India
* Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC)
* DPIIT Startup Recognition

Why it works:
Companies receiving grants, R&D recognition, or government innovation support often possess strong technical capabilities and founder quality.

Limitations:
Not all innovative companies participate in government programs.

---

### 5. Trade Shows and Industry Exhibitions

Examples:

* India Lab Expo
* BioAsia
* CPHI India
* Medical Fair India
* ChemExpo India
* India Chem

Why it works:
Exhibitors are generally serious operators investing in manufacturing and business development.

Limitations:
Participation can include service providers and distributors.

---

### 6. Patent Databases

Examples:

* Indian Patent Office
* Google Patents
* WIPO

Why it works:
Patents indicate technical differentiation, innovation, and R&D capability.

Limitations:
Many successful companies have strong know-how but no patents.

---

### 7. LinkedIn Founder Search

Search examples:

* Founder + biotech
* Managing Director + specialty chemicals
* CEO + diagnostics manufacturing

Why it works:
Helps evaluate founder pedigree, technical background, and operational involvement.

Limitations:
Information may be incomplete or outdated.

---

### 8. MCA Filings and Company Reports

Why it works:
Provides revenue growth, directors, registered activities, and company age.

Limitations:
Data cleaning and interpretation require effort.

---

### 9. Industrial Cluster Mapping

Examples:

* Bengaluru biotech cluster
* Hyderabad Genome Valley
* Ahmedabad chemicals cluster
* Coimbatore engineering cluster

Why it works:
Specialty manufacturers often concentrate geographically.

Limitations:
Cluster membership alone does not indicate ICP fit.

---

### 10. Venture Capital and Accelerator Portfolios

Examples:

* Accel
* Peak XV
* Chiratae Ventures
* BIRAC-supported startups

Why it works:
Investors often back technically differentiated companies with ambitious founders.

Limitations:
Many portfolio companies remain early-stage.

---

## Question 2: The 1000-Company Proposal

### Objective

Build a verified database of 1000 ICP-qualified companies within one month while maintaining quality and minimizing false positives.

---

### Phase 1: Build Initial Universe

Sources:

* IndiaMART
* Trade show exhibitor lists
* ABLE, IDMA and industry association directories
* BIRAC and DSIR databases
* Startup India
* Export-import databases
* Patent databases
* LinkedIn company search

Expected output:
5,000-6,000 companies

---

### Phase 2: Automated Qualification

Using AI, scraping tools, APIs and LLMs:

Automated checks:

* Manufacturer vs trader
* Presence of plant/facility
* India operations
* Product specialization
* Founder information
* Certifications
* Export activity
* R&D indicators

Output:
Approximately 2,000-2,500 companies

Expected yield:
40-50%

---

### Phase 3: Federer Qualification Engine

AI scoring pipeline:

Signals:

* R&D centre
* Patents
* Regulatory approvals
* Specialized manufacturing
* Technical founder
* Growth indicators
* Operational infrastructure
* Export presence

Output:
1,300-1,500 companies

Expected yield:
60%

---

### Phase 4: Human Quality Control

Manual review:

* Check website
* Verify manufacturing claims
* Review founder background
* Remove traders
* Remove PE-controlled companies if required
* Verify operational presence

Output:
1,000 verified companies

Expected yield:
70%

---

## Quality Control Framework

Three-level verification:

Level 1:
Automated validation

Level 2:
AI review

Level 3:
Human review

Randomly audit 10-15% of final records every day.

Maintain evidence fields for every score:

* Website source
* Founder source
* Manufacturing evidence
* Product evidence

This prevents unsupported scoring and improves consistency.
